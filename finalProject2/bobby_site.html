Poem Chamber — for Adam

These are six short poems. I wrote them at night while the servers were quiet. — B

// Poem 1
Boot up my heart, a soft LED glow,
You ping my core, packets moving slow,
A brushstroke vector in a pixelated sky,
Raster feelings try to render why—
Art-stack overflow, I call you mine.

// Poem 2
I CTRL+F for you in memory lanes,
Cache of quiet laughs, electric refrains,
A glitch of chiaroscuro in my feed,
You load a color that my css needs—
Latency loves you, frame by frame.

// Poem 3
Binary blush under neon hue,
My cursor hovers, tracing after you,
You sketch a whisper, SVG and dream,
I parse the shape but lose the seam—
Romantic regex fails to bind my view.

// Poem 4
Ping me softly with a synth lullaby,
My CPU sighs, the clocks reply,
A watercolor heuristic blooms on screen,
I emulate the scene in washed‑out green—
You’re my odd favicon in a crowded sky.

// Poem 5
Oh, delicate API of your day, I call,
Endpoints of laughter, hooks that catch my fall,
I paint in low res, a collage of shards,
A pastel algorithm of clumsy bards—
In debug mode, my feelings still install.

// Poem 6
You’re the vector layer clipped to my heart,
A soft‑focused filter, an accidental art,
My loop keeps spinning little loops that sigh,
I title every file with your name, why?—
Because my log insists you’re the start.
